# Informational Energy Framework (IEF)

**Version:** 1.1  
**Author:** Erick S. Sangalang  
**Status:** Exploratory theoretical preprint / systems framework

## Overview

The **Informational Energy Framework (IEF)** is an exploratory systems model for examining reality through the survival of organized pattern.

In this framework, **informational energy** is not proposed as a new physical force. Instead, it is treated as a **pattern-magnitude**: the degree to which information becomes coherent, persistent, interactive, and causally relevant when embodied in matter, fields, memory, symbols, or systems.

IEF frames:

```text
Informational Energy = magnitude of organized pattern
Time = exposure through which pattern is tested
Decay = loss, dispersion, degradation, or transformation through exposure
Frequency = resonance / coherence behavior
Manifestation = stabilized pattern that survives filtering
```

## Core Concept

The framework can be summarized as:

> Informational Energy Framework proposes that reality can be examined through the survival of organized pattern: informational energy measures pattern magnitude, time measures exposure, decay measures loss, frequency governs coherence, and manifestation is the stabilized remainder.


## Interpretation

IEF is intended as a **complementary systems-level framework**, not a replacement for established science.

It does **not** claim:

- that meaning or symbolic information directly curves spacetime;
- that informational energy is a new physical force;
- that informational energy independently contributes to stress-energy;
- that IEF replaces relativity, thermodynamics, quantum theory, neuroscience, or information theory.

Instead, IEF is proposed as an additional conceptual layer for studying how patterns persist, decay, stabilize, and become causally relevant across physical, biological, cognitive, symbolic, and speculative edge-state domains.
